  1. Egusi Soup

    • Preparartion Time: 3 minutes
    • Serves: After 30 minutes
    • Total:35minutes
    Ingredients Quantity
    Ground Melon 250ml
    Palm Oil 500ml
    Nigerian Dried Pepper One Table Spoon
    Salt To Your Taste
    Chopped Spinach Leaves One Bowl
    Turkey/Fish 1kg

    Step 1: Add ground melon to a bowl, add about a cup of water and mix to form a paste then set aside. Wash and chop
    the spinach, drain in a colander and set aside.
    Step 2: Place a big pan on medium heat, add palm oil and heat for about 3 minutes (Do not bleach oil) add the reserved
    chopped onions and Sautee till translucent.
    Step 3: Add Dried Pepper Mix and stir. Let it boil for 5 minutes.
    Step 4: Add the Ground Melon (egusi paste) in bits to the dried pepper. Reduce the heat and do not stir or cover the pot
    with a lid. Cook for another 10 - 20 minutes.
    Step 5: Add chopped/shredded Turkey or Fish into the soup and gently stir. Add Chopped Spinach Leaves, stir to combine and cook for another 3 to 5 minutes. Take it off the heat, allow to cool for about 10 minutes before serving. Return to contents.

    Source: My Active Kitchen

  2. Afang Soup

    • Preparartion Time: 20 minutes
    • Serves: After 20 minutes
    • Total: 40 minutes
    Ingredients Quantity
    Okazi Leaves 200g
    Water Leaves 300g
    Goat Meat 500g
    Salt To Taste
    Smoked Fish 100g
    Seasoning Cubes 4
    Palm Oil 1/2 Cup
    Ata Rodo Pepper 2

    Step 1: Chop water leaves, rinse and set aside, pound or chop ata rodo pepper and set aside.
    Step 2: Place a pan on medium heat, add washed goat meat, salt, 2 seasoning cubes and onions and bring to boil till
    soft. When the meat is almost well done, add smoked fish and bring to boil for another 5 minutes. You donâ€™t need much
    water to cook Afang soup, so if your stock is much, reduce it by half and add more to the soup if need be.
    Step 3: Add palm oil and bring to boil for about 5 minutes.
    Step 4: Add the pounded okazi leaves, add it to the sauce and continue to boil for another 10-15 minutes. Add also the
    remaining seasoning cubes and salt if need be.
    Step 5: Add water leaves to the soup, stir to combine and cook for another 5 minutes. Take it off the heat and serve with
    swallow of choice. Return to contents.

  3. Jollof Spaghetti

    • Preparartion Time: 5 minutes
    • Serves: After 16 minutes
    • Total: 21 minutes
    Ingredients Quantity
    Spaghetti Pasta of Coice 500g
    Vegetable Oil 1/3 Cup
    Tomato Paste One Sachet
    Salt To Taste
    Smoked Fish 100g
    Seasoning Cubes 2
    Thyme 1 Tea Spoon
    Onions 2 Small Sized

    Step 1: Cook the spaghetti in salted boiling water until al-dente.
    Step 2: Place a pan on medium heat, add vegetable oil and chopped onions. Fry till onions become translucent this
    should take about 2-3 minutes.
    Step 3: Add the tomato paste, salt, curry powder, thyme. Continue to cook on medium-low heat for another 10 minutes.
    Taste and adjust seasoning to taste.
    Step 4: Add cooked spaghetti to the boiling sauce, stir till well combined, reduce the heat so it doesnâ€™t burn. Lastly, add
    the smoked fish and fresh prawns if using and stir to combine..
    Step 5: Leave spaghetti to cook for another 3 minutes or to desired softness (add more spaghetti water if need be). Take
    it off the heat and serve. Return to contents.

  4. Bean Cakes


    • Preparartion Time: 15 minutes
    • Serves: After 30 minutes
    • Total: 45 minutes
    Ingredients Quantity
    Peeled Beans 2 Cups
    Vegetable Oil 2 Cups
    Ata Rodo Pepper 4
    Salt To Taste
    Seasoning Cubes 2
    Onions 1 Big Sized

    Step 1: Blend peeled beans with onions, ata rodo pepper and water to form a thick batter.
    Step 2: If you have a hand mixer, feel free to use it in beating the thick batter and it cuts down the amount of time you
    have to spend in the kitchen.
    Step 3: Add salt to the bean batter to taste.
    Step 4: Place a frying pan on medium heat, add about 2 cups of oil and heat till hot. Test the oil by putting a tiny bit of
    batter in it. The oil should sizzle and batter should float on top, the oil is ready to use.
    Step 5: Scoop batter in bits to the ready hot oil, reduce the heat and cook on each side till golden brown. Repeat the
    process until you exhaust the whole bean batter.
    Step 6: Serve immediately with Akamu (pap), garri or bread (make akara burger). Return to contents.

    Source: My Active Kitchen
